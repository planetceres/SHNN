Summary

This HIT is an academic experiment on text classification.

You will be completing a classification task to determine the relationships between a food item and a drug compound. A pair of food item and drug compounds will be presented to you, along with a sentence taken from medical literature that provides information about how the pair is related.

Task

You will classify the relationship as one of three categories:

  1. Positive
    • The food item increases the efficacy of the drug.
    • The drug helps the absorption of the food item.
    • The food item contains the drug compound.
  2. Negative
    • The food compound decreases the efficacy of the drug.
    • The drug compound inhibits the absorption of the food item.
  3. Neutral
    • There is no indication whether the food item increases or decreases the efficacy of the drug.
    • The food item and the drug compound occur in the same sentence but whether their relationship is positive or negative is undetermined.

Example

Interaction Food item Drug compound Sentence
Negative unsaturated fatty acids isoniazid Both long- and short-term exposure experiments showed that isoniazid inhibited the synthesis of saturated fatty acids greater than C26 and of unsaturated fatty acids greater than C24.
Neutral vitamin D3 digoxin The median for digoxin T(max) was 0.75 h before and after vitamin D3 ingestion.
Positive curcumin GLP-1 Our previous studies demonstrated that curcumin (a yellow pigment of turmeric) significantly increases the secretion of GLP-1 in enteroendocrine L cell line (GLUTag cells).

Instructions

Please drag the slider to the position along the axis that indicates your selection. To the left for "negative", to the right for "positive", and centered for "neutral".

Your selection will be described below the slider, and you should indicate that you are more confident of an answer by moving the slider further to the extremes (i.e. if you think an example pairing is very likely to be positive, you should move the slider to the far right).

You will have exactly 60 seconds to make your selection.
